The Rise and Fall of Adolf Hitler

Timeline of Adolf Hitler's life

  • April 20, 1889: Adolf Hitler is born in Braunau am Inn, Austria.
  • 1907: Hitler moves to Vienna to study art, but is rejected from the Vienna Academy of Fine Arts.
  • 1913: Hitler moves to Munich, Germany.
  • 1914-1918: Hitler serves in the German army during World War I. He is wounded twice and awarded the Iron Cross, First Class.
  • 1919: Hitler joins the German Workers' Party, which later becomes the National Socialist German Workers' Party (Nazi Party)./li>
  • 1921: Hitler becomes the leader of the Nazi Party.
  • 1923: Hitler leads the Beer Hall Putsch, an attempt to overthrow the Bavarian government. The putsch fails and Hitler is imprisoned.
  • 1924: While in prison, Hitler writes Mein Kampf, his political manifesto.
  • 1925: Hitler is released from prison and resumes his leadership of the Nazi Party.
  • 1933: The Nazi Party wins the most seats in the German parliament. Hitler is appointed chancellor of Germany.
  • 1934: Hitler becomes dictator of Germany after the death of President Paul von Hindenburg.
  • 1939: Germany invades Poland, starting World War II.
  • 1941: Germany invades the Soviet Union.
  • 1944: The Allies invade Normandy, France.
  • 1945: Germany surrenders to the Allies. Hitler commits suicide in his bunker in Berlin.
